--- layout: default title: DeepONet for Burgers Equation --- # Learning the 1+2D Burgers Equation with DeepONets This project implements a Deep Operator Network (DeepONet) to learn the solution operator of the 1+2 dimensional viscous Burgers equation. The goal is to learn the mapping: \[ \mathcal{G}: u_0(x,y) \mapsto u(x,y,t) \] where \(u_0(x,y)\) is the initial condition and \(u(x,y,t)\) is the solution of the Burgers PDE. --- ## 1. The PDE We consider the viscous Burgers equation: \[ \partial_t u + u \cdot \nabla u = \nu \Delta u \] where: - \(u = u(x,y,t)\) - \(\nu\) is the viscosity - \(\Delta\) is the Laplacian This nonlinear PDE models convection–diffusion phenomena and shock formation. --- ## 2. Why Operator Learning? Traditional solvers compute: \[ u(x,y,t) \text{ for one initial condition} \] DeepONets aim to learn the **entire solution operator**: \[ \mathcal{G}(u_0) \] Once trained, the network can predict solutions for unseen initial conditions without solving the PDE again. --- ## 3. DeepONet Architecture The model consists of two neural networks: ### Branch Network Encodes the initial condition \(u_0(x,y)\). ### Trunk Network Encodes query coordinates \((x,y,t)\). The final output is: \[ u_\theta(x,y,t) = \sum_{k=1}^{p} b_k(u_0)\, t_k(x,y,t) \] where: - \(b_k\) are branch outputs - \(t_k\) are trunk outputs This structure enables function-to-function learning. --- ## 4. Dataset Generation Training data is generated by: 1. Sampling random smooth initial conditions 2. Solving the Burgers PDE numerically 3. Storing solution snapshots at multiple time steps This produces supervised pairs: \[ (u_0, (x,y,t), u(x,y,t)) \] --- ## 5. Training Objective The model is trained using mean squared error: \[ \mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{N} \sum |u_\theta - u_{\text{true}}|^2 \] Generalization is evaluated on unseen initial conditions. --- ## 6. Results The DeepONet successfully: - Learns nonlinear convection–diffusion dynamics - Generalizes to unseen initial conditions - Captures shock-like structures - Approximates the full operator mapping ### Example Prediction (Add figure here) --- ## 7.
